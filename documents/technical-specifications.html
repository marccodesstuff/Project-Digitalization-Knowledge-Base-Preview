Game Engine

The game will be developed utilizing the Unity game engine, a powerful and versatile platform for creating interactive experiences. Our team has chosen Unity for its robust features, extensive asset store, and cross-platform capabilities. We have plans to transition to Unity 6 upon its official release on October 17, 2024, which will allow us to take advantage of the latest improvements and optimizations offered by this major update.

In terms of rendering technology, we will be implementing the Universal Rendering Pipeline (URP). This choice was made deliberately to ensure optimal performance across a wide range of devices while maintaining high-quality visuals. The URP offers a balance between graphical fidelity and efficiency, making it ideal for our project's requirements.

Currently, our development efforts are focused on creating and refining Unity game objects, with particular emphasis on two critical components: character models and the camera system. The character development process involves intricate modeling, rigging, and animation to bring our game's protagonists and NPCs to life. Simultaneously, we are fine-tuning the camera system to provide smooth, intuitive player perspectives that enhance the overall gaming experience. These elements form the foundation of our game's visual and interactive aspects, and their ongoing development is crucial to achieving our desired gameplay mechanics and aesthetic goals.

Design

For the creation of 3D assets, we will be utilizing Blender, a powerful open-source 3D modeling and animation software. Blender offers a comprehensive suite of tools that will enable us to create high-quality, optimized 3D models for our game.

Key aspects of our Blender workflow include:

  • Low-poly modeling: We'll focus on creating efficient, low-polygon models to ensure smooth performance across various platforms.
  • UV unwrapping: Proper UV mapping will be crucial for applying textures effectively to our 3D models.
  • Rigging and animation: Character rigs and animations will be created directly in Blender, taking advantage of its robust animation toolset. Although for animating some of the complex scenes in the game, the team will be using Unity’s animation system
  • Export pipeline: We'll establish a streamlined export process from Blender to Unity, ensuring proper scale, orientation, and material settings.

By leveraging Blender's capabilities, we aim to create visually appealing and performance-optimized 3D assets that will enhance the overall quality of our game while maintaining compatibility with Unity's Universal Rendering Pipeline.

Music

For the game's soundtrack, we will be utilizing FL Studio as our primary Digital Audio Workstation (DAW) along with FLEX plugins to create a rich and immersive musical experience. Here's an overview of our audio production process:

  • FL Studio: This versatile DAW will serve as the foundation for our music composition and production. Its intuitive interface and powerful features make it ideal for creating game soundtracks.
  • FLEX Plugins: We'll leverage FLEX, FL Studio's powerful synthesizer, to generate a wide range of sounds. FLEX's extensive preset library and sound-shaping capabilities will allow us to create unique and atmospheric sounds tailored to our game's aesthetic.
  • Custom Sound Design: Using FL Studio's native plugins and FLEX, we'll design custom sounds and instruments that align with our game's theme and setting.
  • Adaptive Music System: We'll implement an adaptive music system using FL Studio's playlist feature, allowing the soundtrack to dynamically respond to in-game events and player actions.
  • Mixing and Mastering: FL Studio's mixing console and mastering tools will be used to ensure high-quality audio output that translates well across different playback systems.

By combining FL Studio's robust production environment with the versatility of FLEX plugins, we aim to create a captivating and dynamic soundtrack that enhances the player's immersion and complements the game's visual elements.
